Prison Island

Package Details

Prison Island, also known as Changuu Island, is a hidden gem off the coast of Zanzibar that offers a unique and unforgettable experience. The island's history is shrouded in mystery and intrigue, and its secrets are waiting to be discovered

Initially intended as a prison for rebellious slaves, the island was later used as a quarantine station for yellow fever. Today, it is a popular destination for tourists who come to see its most famous residents - the giant Aldabra tortoises

These gentle giants are some of the oldest and largest tortoises in the world, with some of them weighing over 500 pounds and living for over a century. Visitors can get up close and personal with these majestic creatures and even feed them under the watchful eye of the island's staff

But the island offers more than just giant tortoises. The crystal-clear waters that surround the island are teeming with marine life, making it a prime spot for snorkeling and diving. You can explore the coral reefs, swim with tropical fish, and even spot dolphins and sea turtles in their natural habitat

For history buffs, the island's old prison buildings offer a glimpse into its dark past. The cells where slaves and prisoners were once held are now empty, but the haunting echoes of the past linger on. Visitors can explore these abandoned buildings and learn about the island's complex history

As you explore Prison Island, you'll be struck by its rugged beauty and sense of isolation. It's a place where time seems to stand still, and the only sounds you'll hear are the gentle waves lapping against the shore and the occasional call of a seagull

In many ways, Prison Island is a microcosm of Zanzibar itself - a place of contrasts and contradictions, where beauty and darkness coexist in a delicate balance. A visit to this hidden gem is an experience that will leave you with a newfound appreciation for the resilience and diversity of this incredible island
